## Global Support working with ASEs

Global Support engineers and ASEs work in close partnership to deliver an
exceptional experience for ASE accounts. Global Support owns ticket handling by
default, using org notes and ASE documentation for customer context. ASEs build
and maintain customer documentation for use by Global Support, review the
overall support engagement, conduct ticket trend analysis, and engage directly
based on customer prioritization, current needs, and ticket trends. ASEs also
contribute through regular customer meetings, Slack channels, escalation
coordination, and proactive problem management. This deep customer context helps
Global Support work more effectively.

ASEs may engage directly on specific tickets based on customer priorities or
trends, but ticket handling is not their primary focus. If a customer requests
that their ASE work on or take over a ticket, let the ASE know. The ASE will
decide whether to take ownership or provide supplemental support while Global
Support drives the work. Leave the current assignee in place unless the ASE
explicitly requests to be assigned.

If a ticket assigned to an ASE is approaching an SLA breach and the ASE is
unavailable, respond to the customer directly, leave the ASE assigned, and cc
yourself until they return.

## Working tickets for ASE accounts

When picking up a ticket for an ASE account, start by checking the org note and
the [Assigned Support Engineers](https://gitlab.com/gitlab-com/support/assigned-support-engineers)
project for customer context. ASEs maintain documentation there covering
customer environment details, customer background, and other account-specific
information. Note that some customer context may also be captured directly in
the org note rather than the project, so check both before getting started.

If you need clarification or have questions about the account, reach out on the
ticket itself or in the [#spt_assigned-support-eng](https://gitlab.enterprise.slack.com/archives/C06E61D9Z7E)
Slack channel. ASEs and ASE leaders are available to provide context and
guidance.

### Emergency tickets

Regardless of when an emergency request is submitted by an ASE's account, the
[DRI](/handbook/people-group/directly-responsible-individuals/) for the
emergency is the [on-call support engineer](/handbook/support/workflows/customer_emergencies_workflows/).
The only process change is that the on-call support engineer should **notify the ASE**.
The best way to do this is simply to ping the ASE's Slack handle in the emergency's Slack
thread. After that, the on-call engineer should work the emergency in the normal way.

Guidance for ASEs regarding emergencies from their accounts can be found on the
[ASE emergencies page](/handbook/support/enhanced-support-offerings/offering-assigned-support-engineer/emergencies/)

## Covering for an ASE when they are absent

ASEs arrange their own PTO coverage following the [ASE PTO planning workflow](/handbook/support/enhanced-support-offerings/offering-assigned-support-engineer/ase-workflows-and-standards/planning-pto-as-ase/).
In some cases, they may ask a Global Support engineer to help cover their accounts.
